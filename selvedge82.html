Selvedge Magazine // Issue 82: Lace

Lace is a lesson in contradiction. It is demure, erotic, delicate, brutal, cheap, sophisticated, fragile and strong. It's made with needle and thread; with miniature bobbins; and with cast iron looms weighing more than ten tons.

In the 19th century lace instigated the Luddite riots, and catalysed the smuggling of looms across borders. It is also the darling of the haute couture fashion industry and a source of ridicule when used to shield prying eyes in suburban homes.

This issue unpicks this complex material, visualising the working of Leaver looms, tracing its history, and examining its political status in the form of the veil.
