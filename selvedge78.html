Selvedge Magazine // Issue 78: Substance

This issue of Selvedge celebrates the material empathy of today's makers and designers. Since the post-war world, synthetic fibres were seen as saviours that would liberate us from the toil of laundry and the inflexibility of natural fibre.

But as time passed and experience gained, breathability and biodegradability became increasingly important, shifting the material hierarchy that today's textile industry was built on.

Issue 78 looks to the innovative work of Christien Meindertsma, the history of the Pennsylvania Dutch settlers, the biography of the bag and the state of endangered materials to find out in this issue, why substance matters.
