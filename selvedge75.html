Selvedge Magazine // Issue 75: Endeavour

This issue of Selvedge Magazine discovers the true benefits of work. From the life-saving lace of 19th century Ireland, to the revival of the French worker's jacket, this issue looks at how we work with fabric, and how it works with us in return.

As the cost of higher education rises, we see a movement towards the workbench which brings us back to our past, and examine apprenticeship schemes set up after World War One.

From the health benefits of work to its economic, social and political impacts, the stories in this issue arrive at America's largest mills, at Japanese denim and at Mourne Textiles, where a great woman's work is being recovered.
