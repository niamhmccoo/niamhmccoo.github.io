Selvedge Magazine // Issue 80: Craft

2017 saw the unveiling of the Radcliffe Red list: a list that identified crafts that are viable, endangered, critically endangered and extinct in the UK. This issue of Selvedge Magazine analyses its impact.

Hilary Burns introduces the surprising role that basket making played in World War One: in the mass production of shell baskets, and the development of occupational therapy for wounded and shell-shocked soldiers.

And by venturing to Iceland where craft is ingrained into the eduction systems, we can imagine what would be possible if we had something like this for craft in the rest of the world.
